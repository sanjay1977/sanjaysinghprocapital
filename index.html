Setup

Every trade begins with structure. I only act when the setup matches my predefined plan—no gut entries, no emotional triggers. Entry, stop loss, and target are locked before execution. If the setup isn’t clean, I don’t touch it.

Reflection

After every trade, I log the mindset, not just the result. Was I calm? Was I aligned? The archive holds these reflections as proof—not performance. This loop of review protects my clarity and sharpens my discipline.

Trading Psychology

Markets test emotion more than strategy. I trade only when I’m emotionally neutral—no FOMO, no revenge, no distraction. Discipline is the filter. If I’m tired, reactive, or drifting, I step back. My edge is clarity, not urgency.
