Trading Psychology

I don’t chase price. I listen to it. If it’s in my zone, I act. If not, I wait.

Breakouts and breakdowns are noise. Zones are truth.

My setup speaks when the market is silent.

Time is rhythm, not urgency. 5:30 AM is a checkpoint, not a trigger.

I trade inside clarity, not outside emotion.

Decision Loop

  • Observe: Price at 5:30 AM IST. Is it inside my zone?
  • Align: If in-zone, listen for setup. If outside, wait.
  • Act: Execute only on confirmation. Fixed stop and target.
  • Reflect: Log zone, setup, result, and one sentence of truth.

Zone Logic

  • Zones are drawn from prior decision points—rejections, consolidations, fair-value areas.
  • Setup types: Wick rejection, Sweep-return, Structure shift, Momentum candle.
  • Invalidation: Clean close beyond zone edge or time expiration without setup.

Execution Discipline

  • Entry only inside zone, only on setup.
  • Stop beyond invalidation—never widened.
  • Target: next zone, key level, or session boundary.
  • Fixed risk per trade. No mid-trade edits.

Daily Rhythm

  • Pre-market: Draw zones. Define primary and secondary setups.
  • 5:30 AM IST: Check price. Is it in-zone? Is setup forming?
  • Execution window: Act only if setup confirms. Else, stand down.
  • Post-trade: Log with one sentence of truth. Archive the decision.
